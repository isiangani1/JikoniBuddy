Project Summary Project name: Jikoni Buddy Launch location: Nairobi first, then expand city by city Marketplace services: meal prep, office bites, picnic snacks (MVP focus). Catering and other food services can be listed, but are secondary for MVP. Core concept: a trusted food services marketplace with buyer, seller, and admin portals Key differentiator: Buddy Pool, a feature that lets sellers request extra helpe rs when they are short staffed Stage: pre-product. Documentation is in progress and will be frozen after early validation. 3. Platforms Required Responsive web app Android app iOS app 4. Payments M-Pesa payment (STK Push or equivalent) Pay on delivery opti on (recorded and confirmed in the system) 5. Non Negotiable MVP Requirements Scheduled orders from day 1 (buyers can choose a future date and time window) Seller availability and lead time rules (seller can set availability, lead times, and blackout times) Order status workflow (placed, accepted, in preparation, out for delivery, delivered, completed) Client owned accounts and codebase: GitHub repository must be in the Client account from day 1 Weekly demo and weekly code push 6. Buyer Portal MVP Scope Sign up and login (phone or email) Browse sellers by category, location, rating, availability Seller profile: services offered, menu items, pricing, lead times, service area Place an order with scheduled date and time window, notes, and delivery address Choose payment method: M-Pesa or pay on delivery Order tracking with status updates Order messaging or chat linked to an order (can be basic) Ratings and reviews after completion Support entry point (ticket or WhatsApp link) 7. Seller Portal MVP Scope Sell er onboarding: profile, categories, delivery areas, lead times, availability Menu management: items, pricing, photos, availability toggles Order management: accept or reject, update status, message buyer Payout view: record of completed orders and amounts owed (even if payouts are handled manually at first) Buddy Pool: seller can request helper(s) by date, time, task type, and location (basic request flow) 8. Admin Portal MVP Scope Admin login and role permissions Seller approval and verification checklist View and manage all orders and statuses Dispute and refund handling (record actions and outcomes) Fee or commission settings (simple in MVP) Seller quality actions: warnings, suspensions, removal Basic metrics dashboard: orders, GMV, cancellations, issues, top sellers 9. Trust, Quality, and Disputes (MVP) Seller verification process (lightweight: documents upload optional, admin checklist required) Reviews policy: only after a completed order Seller performance score inputs: reliabilit y, cancellations, response time, ratings Support and dispute flow: evidence collection, timelines, admin decisions, refund rules 10. Delivery (MVP) Delivery fee logic: simple zone based or distance based pricing Ability to handle seller delivery vs rider delivery (flag per seller or per order) Status updates: out for delivery and delivered 11. Developer Deliverables Full source code in Client GitHub with commit history Setup guide: local setup, build steps, deployment steps Admin guide: how to approv e sellers, manage orders, handle disputes and refunds Dependency list and licenses (open source compliance) App store submission support for Android and iOS 12. Quotation Format Required Total cost for web plus Android plus iOS Cost breakdown per milestone Timeline per milestone (weeks) Recommended tech stack and architecture summary Support or warranty period after launch (30 to 60 days) and what it includes What is excluded and any assumptions Team composition and responsibilities (backend, mobile, UI, QA ) 13.
